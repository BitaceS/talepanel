Typical use cases that require a commercial license:

- Game hosting providers offering TalePanel as a managed service to their customers.
- White-labelled deployments where the control-plane source cannot be published.
- Embedding TalePanel into a closed-source product.

Private, non-commercial self-hosting (running TalePanel for your own Hytale community or clan) does **not** require a commercial license. The AGPL-3.0 covers you.

## How to obtain a commercial license

Email **licensing@talepanel.com** with:

- Your company name and country.
- The rough scope of use (single-tenant vs. multi-tenant hosting, approximate node count).
- Whether you need source-disclosure exemption only, or also redistribution rights.

A commercial license is granted on a per-deployment or per-seat basis depending on scale. Pricing is transparent and published on `talepanel.com/pricing` once the commercial program is live.

## What a commercial license grants

- Waiver of the AGPL-3.0 Section 13 (network source-disclosure) obligations for the licensed deployment.
- Permission to integrate TalePanel into a closed-source product.
- Access to security patches in a private channel before public disclosure (for the active license period).
- Priority support with a response-time SLA (separate from the license itself — sold as an add-on).

A commercial license does **not** grant:

- The right to re-license TalePanel to third parties.
- The right to remove the copyright notices or license files from the source.
